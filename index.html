Kako se je vse začelo.

The cigarette fit nicely with the shadows that morning. It was 14 minutes after 8, and the rhythm of him appearing into his consciousness reminded me of a syncope, a little off-beat. The working class was already in their places, and he simply sat there, carrying nothing more than a black leather backpack, with one earphone in his ear and smoke caressing the sunny side of his face. He looked straight through me. The train was approaching, but he remained looking at me.

---

I dozed off in the wagon with my book left open when I noticed him sitting across from me, absently staring into moving trees.

Cigareta se je tistega jutra lepo podala sencam. Bilo je 14 minut čez osmo in ritem njegovega pojavljanja v zavest me je spominjal na sinkopo, nekoliko zunaj ritma. Delavski razred je bil že na svojih mestih, on pa je preprosto sedel tam, brez česar koli drugega kot črnega usnjenega nahrbtnika, z eno slušalko v ušesu in dimom, ki je božal sončno stran njegovega obraza. Gledal je naravnost skozi mene. Vlak se je bližal, a on je še naprej gledal vame.

---

Zadremal sem v vagonu z odprto knjigo, ko sem opazil, da sedi nasproti mene in odsotno strmi v premikajoča se drevesa.

There was nobody in that compartment but the two of us. It felt almost like being in a room with an untamed animal covered in human skin; he was being called to something. He put on his sunglasses, earphones silently playing a steady tune, tilted his head a bit, and was away into his own mind.

The ticket lady arrived shortly afterward. He had already left his ticket in the open to be checked. No one disturbed him, as if they were informed of his journey. I noticed he carried a camera with him. I couldn’t help but wonder: what does a person like that take pictures of?

V tistem kupeju ni bilo nikogar razen naju. Zdelo se je skoraj tako, kot bi bil v sobi z neukročeno živaljo, prekrito s človeško kožo; nekaj ga je klicalo. Nadel si je sončna očala, slušalke so tiho igrale enakomerno melodijo, nagnil je glavo in odtaval v svoj um.

Kmalu zatem je prišla sprevodnica. Svojo vozovnico je že pustil na vidnem mestu za pregled. Nihče ga ni motil, kot da bi bili obveščeni o njegovem potovanju. Opazil sem, da ima s seboj fotoaparat. Nisem si mogel kaj, da se ne bi vprašal: kaj takšna oseba fotografira?

He didn’t move for three straight hours; his sunglasses gave the impression he was constantly observing his surroundings. His breathing was reduced to silence. I finished reading my book. The police border was approaching, and I knew he could sense that too.

“It must have been a good book,” he remarked.

“Excuse me?” I asked.

“Oh, yes. It was indeed.” I responded honestly.

The doors opened loudly, and one police officer checked our documents. They didn’t ask him anything; he didn’t even remove his sunglasses.

“When you travel, leave your luggage slightly open—you won’t seem so suspicious next time,” he said politely.

Tri ure se ni premaknil; njegova sončna očala so dajala vtis, da nenehno opazuje okolico. Njegovo dihanje je bilo zmanjšano na tišino. Končal sem branje knjige. Bližala se je policijska meja in vedel sem, da to čuti tudi on.

“Verjetno je bila dobra knjiga,” je pripomnil.

“Oprostite?” sem vprašal.

“Oh, da. Res je bila. Res je bila,” sem iskreno odgovoril.

Vrata so se glasno odprla in policist je pregledal najine dokumente. Njega niso ničesar vprašali; niti sončnih očal ni snel.

“Ko potujete, pustite prtljago rahlo odprto – naslednjič ne boste videti tako sumljivi,” je vljudno rekel.

“They’re just doing their job. Humans. Same as you and me. But give them a sense of power, and you’ll really get to know them. Thank you, young man. I will try your method next time.”

A lovely young lady in an oversized yellow coat invited herself into our compartment. She fell asleep after a couple of train stops. Scribbling away into my thoughts, I saw that the young man had a delicate tear coming out of the corner of his eye.

“Are you alright, young man?” I asked silently.

He just looked at me and smiled. “It’s Lacrimosa, from Zbigniew Preisner. I do not understand why, but sometimes I have a feeling it cuts deep inside my chest and releases me from my body.”

“Samo opravljajo svoje delo. Ljudje. Enaki kot vi in jaz. Toda dajte jim občutek moči in res jih boste spoznali. Hvala, mladenič. Naslednjič bom poskusil vašo metodo.”

Ljubka mladenka v prevelikem rumenem plašču se je povabila v najin kupe. Po nekaj postajah je zaspala. Ko sem čečkal svoje misli, sem videl, da ima mladenič v kotičku očesa nežno solzo.

“Ste v redu, mladenič?” sem tiho vprašal.

Samo pogledal me je in se nasmehnil. “To je Lacrimosa, Zbigniew Preisner. Ne razumem zakaj, a včasih imam občutek, da mi zareže globoko v prsi in me osvobodi telesa.”

“You are a writer, I presume?” he read me at a glance.

“You might say that, yes.”

“I always wanted to tell someone my story.”

“Well, it’s a long trip ahead of us. I’m Jonathan.”

“Kint,” he nodded.

The train lights went out. Somehow, the power was out. In the distance, we saw lightning. The train was stranded. “Sehr geehrte Fahrgäste...” The woman’s voice spoke politely with a slightly worried undertone. Something loud sounded almost like two gunshots in front of the train.

“Predvidevam, da ste pisatelj?” me je prebral z enim pogledom.

“Lahko bi tako rekli, da.”

“Vedno sem želel nekomu povedati svojo zgodbo.”

“No, pred nami je dolga pot. Jaz sem Jonathan.”

“Kint,” je prikimal.

Luči na vlaku so ugasnile. Zmanjkalo je elektrike. V daljavi smo videli bliskanje. Vlak je obtičal. “Sehr geehrte Fahrgäste...” Ženski glas je govoril vljudno z rahlo zaskrbljenim podtonom. Nekaj glasnega je zvenelo skoraj kot dva strela pred vlakom.

Serpentine descending down a mountain, military boots grinding along the unpaved road. Currents flow in the background of silence. All operators in position. The plan was simple: destabilize the country, offer them aid, install the operators, and overtake.

Some have foreseen this ancient method. That’s where our units came into play. We called them platforms. Psychological operations have proven decisive in the past. Rootkits in the subconscious ticked exactly as we wanted them to. Their thoughts below the surface had been planted, reinforced, and used predictably for our purposes. We named them “sleepers.” We transformed them into devices capable of executing commands via radio frequency signals. Other triggers included music, photos, and the most effective stimuli—smell.

Force and blackmail brought us to a certain level of efficiency, but over time, resistance was better subdued through conditioning and platforms. Almost all entry points—we called them “doors”—were accessed through trust, evoking love, and exploiting the raw tissue of the psyche by inducing individuals or groups into chronic stress and flooding them with information.

Serpentina se je spuščala po gori, vojaški škornji so mleli po neasfaltirani cesti. Tokovi so tekli v ozadju tišine. Vsi operaterji na položajih. Načrt je bil preprost: destabilizirati državo, ji ponuditi pomoč, namestiti operaterje in prevzeti oblast.

Nekateri so predvideli to starodavno metodo. Tu so nastopile naše enote. Imenovali smo jih platforme. Psihološke operacije so se v preteklosti izkazale za odločilne. "Rootkiti" v podzavesti so tiktakali točno tako, kot smo želeli. Njihove misli pod površjem so bile vsajene, okrepljene in predvidljivo uporabljene za naše namene. Imenovali smo jih "speči". Spremenili smo jih v naprave, sposobne izvrševanja ukazov preko radijskih frekvenčnih signalov. Drugi sprožilci so vključevali glasbo, fotografije in najučinkovitejši dražljaj – vonj.

Sila in izsiljevanje sta nas pripeljala do določene stopnje učinkovitosti, a sčasoma je bil odpor bolje zatrt s pogojevanjem in platformami. Skoraj vse vstopne točke – imenovali smo jih "vrata" – so bile dostopne prek zaupanja, zbujanja ljubezni in izkoriščanja surovega tkiva psihe s potiskanjem posameznikov ali skupin v kronični stres in poplavo informacij.

The goal was to make them susceptible to C programming while strongly distracted by a task. Usually, subjects succumbed to this suggestion within three to five months. Sleep deprivation accelerated the process. We knew the program was active when the subject woke up every night at 3 a.m., dreaming of the structures and triggers we installed. The programs started losing their effect after three months of losing contact with the inductor.

Three psychological platforms were commonly used: one labeled “Parent,” the second “Adult,” and the third “Child”—also called “Ego states.” This was a modified version of Eric Berne’s transactional analysis, adapted for covert operations by the special units of leading countries and some influential corporations.

A priest was used as a vector for triggers. Their training made them resistant to three of the five methods employed. It was my specialty to infiltrate systems. My first goal was to gather intelligence and clear any personal interference.

Cilj je bil narediti jih dovzetne za C programiranje, medtem ko so bili močno raztreseni z drugo nalogo. Običajno so subjekti podlegli tej sugestiji v treh do petih mesecih. Pomanjkanje spanja je pospešilo proces. Vedeli smo, da je program aktiven, ko se je subjekt zbujal vsako noč ob treh zjutraj in sanjal o strukturah ter sprožilcih, ki smo jih namestili.

Uporabljale so se tri psihološke platforme: ena z oznako "Starš", druga "Odrasel" in tretja "Otrok" – imenovana tudi "Ego stanja". To je bila modificirana različica transakcijske analize Erica Berna, prilagojena za tajne operacije.

Duhovnik je bil uporabljen kot vektor za sprožilce. Njihovo usposabljanje jih je naredilo odporne na tri od petih uporabljenih metod. Moja specialnost je bila infiltracija v sisteme.

One of our operatives discovered shortly before a mission that both his parents had burned to death in an elderly home. Despite this trauma, his performance was superb, and he maintained his inductor role for another six months. His state served as a model for the X9 unit training. We entered CRISPR tissue and DNA manipulation fused with nano-bio processors. We achieved a collective awareness among units—they operated as a single consciousness.

We kept entire units in abandoned Buddhist temples as “sleeper cells.” The number of active processes in Europe approached 1,300. Countries had been overtaken from the inside out, working their way from administration to the political core.

There were two rebel groups aware of this movement, one of which was led by the former sleeper. He exhibited an unprecedented level of awareness. The firm became aware of him when he was arrested and isolated on charges of forming a sect in a remote mountain village.

Eden od naših operativcev je tik pred misijo odkril, da sta oba njegova starša zgorela v domu za ostarele. Kljub tej travmi je bila njegova izvedba vrhunska. Njegovo stanje je služilo kot model za usposabljanje enote X9. Vstopili smo v manipulacijo tkiva in DNA s CRISPR, združeno z nano-bio procesorji. Dosegli smo kolektivno zavest med enotami.

Cele enote smo zadrževali v zapuščenih budističnih templjih kot "speče celice". Število aktivnih procesov v Evropi se je bližalo 1300.

Obstajali sta dve uporniški skupini, ki sta se zavedali tega gibanja; eno je vodil nekdanji speči agent. Izkazoval je raven zavedanja brez primere.

No one heard from them for three years until members appeared simultaneously in groups of three across 50 capitals worldwide. They simply stood still for exactly twenty-four hours, then disappeared into the crowds like ghosts. We kept him under observation, and after six days, the guards began believing they were him. Highly trained operatives with psy training lasted only nine days before showing signs of a security breach.

“Your use of code is childish. You leave the platforms unguarded. If I can access them in two days, they can do it in under seven hours.”

“Who are they?”

He did not respond.

His use of an invented language—an altered form of neuro-linguistic programming and code-switching—was his signature. He managed to escape from an underground research facility on German soil. Cameras showed he was accompanied by three guards, walking at exactly the same distance from each other, with him in the middle. He vanished. His team was never identified again.

Tri leta ni nihče slišal o njih, dokler se niso člani pojavili istočasno v skupinah po tri v 50 prestolnicah po svetu. Preprosto so stali pri miru točno 24 ur, nato pa izginili v množici kot duhovi.

Imeli smo ga pod nadzorom in po šestih dneh so stražarji začeli verjeti, da so on. Visoko usposobljeni operativci so zdržali le devet dni.

“Vaša uporaba kode je otročja. Platforme puščate nezavarovane. Če lahko jaz dostopam do njih v dveh dneh, lahko oni to storijo v manj kot sedmih urah.”

“Kdo so oni?”

Ni odgovoril.

Uspel je pobegniti iz podzemnega raziskovalnega objekta na nemških tleh. Kamere so pokazale, da so ga spremljali trije stražarji, ki so hodili na točno enaki razdalji drug od drugega. Izginil je.

All forces led double lives. On one side, we played gods; privately, we concealed this world from ourselves. I often stayed in a beach bar to enjoy normality, knowing I could never fully return once I’d glimpsed the other side. None of us understood the depths of our applications—that was the true thrill.

Before me stood a pint of beer with a generous pour. Behind it, the girl working as the bartender looked at me with concern.

“It’s well over the line,” I said appreciatively. I made her laugh. She was the boss’s daughter, recently finished high school and entering a new chapter. I noticed her distant awareness of her role as a bartender. You can always tell who was cut out for something “more” in their lives.

The girl’s jeans were dangerously short. Her golden, curly hair stood out like a rebellious statement. One morning, I woke up under the bar with a glass of water and a note beside me. It read, “My shift was over. Call me when you wake up.”

Her name was Natalia. I visited the bar after a week and learned she had quit.

Vse sile so živele dvojno življenje. Po eni strani smo se igrali bogove; zasebno smo ta svet skrivali pred samimi seboj. Pogosto sem ostajal v baru na plaži, da bi užival v normalnosti. Nihče od nas ni razumel globin naših aplikacij – to je bil pravi čar.

Pred menoj je stal vrček piva z velikodušno mero. Za njim me je zaskrbljeno gledalo dekle, ki je delalo kot natakarica.

“Krepko čez črto,” sem rekel s hvaležnostjo. Nasmejal sem jo. Bila je šefova hči. Opazil sem njeno oddaljeno zavedanje svoje vloge. Vedno lahko prepoznaš tiste, ki so ustvarjeni za nekaj "več".

Njene kavbojke so bile nevarno kratke. Nekega jutra sem se zbudil pod točilnim pultom s kozarcem vode in sporočilom: "Moja izmena se je končala. Pokliči me, ko se zbudiš."

Ime ji je bilo Natalia. Po tednu dni sem obiskal bar in izvedel, da je dala odpoved.

That evening, I walked home for an hour to clear my mind. I noticed an unanswered call and a message just before arriving at the agency-managed apartment: “Can I see you today? It’s urgent.”

I’d never had this kind of relationship with her, but it was as if she knew no introductions were necessary. I replied, “Yeah, come on over.”

“You should stop mixing tequila with beer; you’re not the type.”

“What type do I strike you as?”

“Sober, gentle. Sipping the heavy surface of red wine. You fell in love with me. You’re easy to read.”

I was stunned at how obvious everything had become.

“I may have killed a man. He’s still lying in the hallway, bleeding from his head... I’m kidding. Just wanted to see how you’d react. There’s a small meeting today with my roommate. Want to meet her?”

“It’ll be fun, I promise. Besides, we already slept together that night you passed out at the bar.”

Tisti večer sem eno uro hodil domov, da bi si zbistril misli. Tik preden sem prispel v stanovanje, sem opazil neodgovorjen klic in sporočilo: “Te lahko vidim danes? Nujno je.”

Nikoli nisem imel takšnega odnosa z njo, a bilo je, kot da bi vedela, da predstavitve niso potrebne. Odgovoril sem: “Ja, pridi.”

“Moral bi nehati mešati tekilo s pivom; nisi tak tip.”

“Kakšen tip pa se ti zdim?”

“Trezen, nežen. Tak, ki srka težko gladino rdečega vina. Zaljubil si se vame. Lahko te je brati.”

“Morda sem ubila človeka. Še vedno leži na hodniku, krvavi iz glave... Hecam se. Samo hotela sem videti, kako boš reagiral. Danes imam majhen sestanek s sostanovalko. Jo želiš spoznati?”

“Zabavno bo, obljubim. Poleg tega sva že spala skupaj tisto noč, ko si v baru izgubil zavest.”

“What are you doing? I’ll have to decline. I have work in the morning.”

“No, you don’t. I know what you do. But that’s irrelevant now.”

“You had him locked up in your facility for eight days. At least, that’s what you believed. He wasn’t locked up; he wasn’t even there.”

I pulled out my firearm and pressed her against the wall. “Start talking!”

She kissed me and grabbed my crotch. “I am the new recruit. Security clearance is Aurora-7.”

“We have an update on Cologne. The squad followed through with the procedure. However, we believe Kint acted out of plan. We need to contain the damage.”

“She’s here.”

A formal-looking middle-aged woman appeared at the door. Her perfectly trimmed hair suggested strictness, but her first smile shattered that assumption.

“God, I hate this cover—a fucking librarian again. I’m Rosemarie—you can call me Rose. Natalie was just having her fun. Her specialty, actually. She gathers good data.”

“Kaj delaš? Moral bom zavrniti. Zjutraj imam delo.”

“Ne, nimaš dela. Vem, kaj delaš. A to je zdaj nepomembno.”

“Osem dni ste ga imeli zaprtega v svojem objektu. Vsaj tako ste verjeli. Ni bil zaprt; sploh ga ni bilo tam.”

Izvlekel sem orožje in jo pritisnil ob steno. “Govori!”

Poljubila me je in me zgrabila za mednožje. “Jaz sem nova rekrutka. Varnostno dovoljenje je Aurora-7.”

“Imamo nove informacije o Kölnu. Enota je izvedla postopek. Vendar verjamemo, da je Kint ravnal mimo načrta. Škodo moramo omejiti.”

Na vratih se je pojavila urejena ženska srednjih let. Njena popolna pričeska je nakazovala strogost, a njen prvi nasmeh je to domnevo razblinil.

“Bog, sovražim to krinko – spet prekleta knjižničarka. Jaz sem Rosemarie – lahko me kličeš Rose.”

As I poured wine, I glimpsed Rose undressing to remove wearable electronics. I noticed scars—communication mods. Natalie performed a penetration test on my laptop while I was in the kitchen.

“You need to be more careful. It was a coordinated inside job with an inside man. We suspect Kint,” Rose added.

“Kint is just like all of us—gifted in some field, with a difficult past. Some even called him a prophet. However, predicting women proved difficult—that’s where I came in.”

“Would you describe him as a sadist?” Rose asked.

“He wasn’t exactly a sadist, but his performance was an art form.”

“We need to extract some information from you,” Natalie replied with a sly smile.

Rose began undressing herself as if she had all the time in the world. Natalie slipped Rose onto me. They kissed passionately, as if lovers who hadn’t seen each other in months. In all my years of service, I’d never experienced anything like this.

Ko sem točil vino, sem opazil, da se Rose slači, da bi odstranila nosljivo elektroniko. Opazil sem brazgotine – komunikacijske modifikacije. Natalie je medtem izvedla penetracijski test na mojem prenosniku.

“Biti moraš bolj previden. Šlo je za usklajen napad od znotraj. Sumimo Kinta,” je dodala Rose.

“Kint je tak kot vsi mi – nadarjen na določenem področju, s težko preteklostjo. Nekateri so ga celo klicali prerok. Vendar se je napovedovanje žensk izkazalo za težavno – tu sem vskočil jaz.”

“Bi ga opisali kot sadista?” je vprašala Rose.

“Ni bil ravno sadist, ampak videl sem, česa je sposoben. Njegova izvedba je bila umetnost.”

Rose se je začela slačiti, kot da ima ves čas na svetu. Natalie je spustila Rose name. Strastno sta se poljubljali, kot ljubimki, ki se nista videli mesece.

When I woke, a croissant, orange juice, and a pot of coffee awaited on a tray on the table. They were gone without a trace. Under the coffee cup, I found a small note. It read: “Rosebud.” It was Kint.

“Rosebud” was the codename for the operation that cleared a hospital in Russia. Today marked exactly ten years since it happened. For a moment, I felt sorrow. Many civilians had lost their lives that night as collateral damage. Kint had saved two operatives from bleeding out. Fuck, I can’t believe he remembered. He even gave the recruits the clearance number—typical Kint.

Nobody ever discovered what part of his training he’d undergone. Rumor has it he fought his personal demons for a long time before joining the forces. He botched his first mission, lost a female operative, and descended into madness. Nobody heard from him for three years.

Ko sem se zbudil, so me na pladnju na mizi čakali rogljiček, pomarančni sok in kava. Izginili sta brez sledu. Pod skodelico kave sem našel majhen listek. Pisalo je: “Rosebud”. Bil je Kint.

“Rosebud” je bilo kodno ime za operacijo čiščenja bolnišnice v Rusiji. Danes je minilo točno deset let, odkar se je to zgodilo. Za trenutek sem začutil žalost. Kint je rešil dva operativca pred izkrvavitvijo. Prekletstvo, ne morem verjeti, da si je zapomnil. Rekrutom je dal celo varnostno številko – tipičen Kint.

Nihče ni nikoli odkril, kakšen del usposabljanja je prestal. Govori se, da se je dolgo boril z osebnimi demoni, preden se je pridružil silam. Svojo prvo misijo je zavozil, izgubil operativko in zdrsel v norost. Tri leta ni nihče slišal zanj.

He was seen on a mountain top, rescued by local climbers. They reported he was dehydrated, delusional, with an obvious intent to end his life there. After that incident, he began developing his artistic side—music and intensive therapy helped revive what was left of him. After recovery, he fell in love. A calmness accompanied him from that moment. I didn’t recognize him at first; he was later promoted into cyber units.

It seems he knows what everyone in the firm is doing—something like a patron with a dark past. His trademark was often changing camouflage and moving swiftly between different circles. His wife described him as a Greek god Hermes—living between worlds of life and death, appearing wherever he was needed most.

The Cologne mission was staged. All “victims” on board were our new recruits—they played dead with state-of-the-art effects. The footage was used as leverage to expose the underground hierarchy.

Videli so ga na gorskem vrhu, rešili so ga lokalni plezalci. Poročali so, da je bil dehidriran, bloden in z očitnim namenom, da tam konča svoje življenje. Po tem incidentu je začel razvijati svojo umetniško plat. Po okrevanju se je zaljubil. Od tistega trenutka ga je spremljala mirnost. Sprva ga nisem prepoznal.

Zdi se, da ve, kaj počnejo vsi v podjetju – kot nekakšen patron s temno preteklostjo. Njegov zaščitni znak je bilo pogosto menjavanje kamuflaže in hitro gibanje med različnimi krogi. Njegova žena ga je opisala kot grškega boga Hermesa – živel je med svetovi življenja in smrti ter se pojavljal tam, kjer je bil najbolj potreben.

Misija v Kölnu je bila zrežirana. Vse "žrtve" na krovu so bili naši novi rekruti – igrali so mrtve z uporabo najsodobnejših efektov. Posnetki so bili uporabljeni kot vzvod za razkritje podzemne hierarhije.

Music from R.E.M. played through the vinyl record in a rustic teahouse. The Luger was loaded, waiting to fire. People started falling from their seats upon noticing the gun. The mask covered my face. The 70-year-old son of Reich’s security official looked me sharply in the eyes before being eliminated—two bullets in his torso and four in his head. I removed the two last bullets from the barrel as a reminder of a job well done. Witnesses shrank back in fear. I heard screams as I left the café. Heavy rain began falling as I slipped into a side alley. It felt cleansing.

I entered the church—a good place to gather my thoughts for a report. A priest appeared beside me as I reflected on the event.

“You did well, my son. The passage is under the altar. Evac-team and new IDs are waiting on the other side. Did you keep the last bullet?”

“Of course, Father.”

“Godspeed.”

Glasba skupine R.E.M. se je vrtela na vinilni plošči v rustikalni čajnici. Luger je bil napolnjen, pripravljen na strel. Ljudje so začeli padati s sedežev, ko so opazili pištolo. Maska mi je prekrivala obraz. 70-letni sin rajhovega varnostnega uradnika me je ostro pogledal v oči, preden je bil eliminiran. Odstranil sem zadnja dva naboja iz cevi kot spomin na dobro opravljeno delo. Ko sem zapuščal kavarno, sem slišal krike. Začel je padati močan dež. Delovalo je očiščujoče.

Vstopil sem v cerkev – dober kraj za zbiranje misli za poročilo. Ob meni se je pojavil duhovnik.

“Dobro si opravil, sin moj. Prehod je pod oltarjem. Evakuacijska ekipa in nova identiteta te čakata na drugi strani. Si obdržal zadnji naboj?”

“Seveda, oče.”

“Srečno pot.”
